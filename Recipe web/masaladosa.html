Masala Dosa

Type :- Vegetarian

Style :- South Indian

Cooking Time :- 10 minutes

Best Served With :- Sambar, Coconut Chutney, Potato Palya

Varieties :- Mysore Masala Dosa, Davangere Masala Dosa etc.

Description

Masala Dosa is a popular South Indian breakfast where a crispy crepe made of fermented rice and lentil batter is served with flavorful spiced potato curry. It is a wholesome meal in itself as it is served with potato masala, Coconut chutney and Sambar.

It is believed that Masala dosa originated in the Tuluva Mangalorean cuisine, from Karnataka (source: Wiki). Udupi restaurants made this toothsome and delectable food very popular all over India and now these are also popular in many countries.

Most South Indian households make dosas regularly and each home has their own recipe. Making a home style masala dosa is super simple as it can be made with any plain dosa batter. You donâ€™t even need a sambar and chutney as the potato masala itself is good enough.

Ingredients

  1. Rice :- Long gran rice.
  2. Avalakki(Poha).
  3. Bele(Dal) :- Uddin bele(Urad dal), Kadale bele(Chana dal), or Togari bele(Piegon peas).
  4. Water.

Procedure

  1. Wash and soak the rice, uddin bele(urad dal), and mentya beeja(fenugreek seeds) overnight.
  2. Grind the soaked ingredients.
  3. Ferment the batter.
  4. Add required(little) amount of salt and soda powder.
  5. Now pour the batter evenly on the pan in thin round shape.
  6. Turn around the dosa after it is cooked one side.
  7. Now after it is well cooked both the sides, the masala dosa is ready to eat.

Serve With

  1. Sambar.
  2. Potato Palya(Potato cury).
  3. Cocunut chutney.

Variants

  1. Mysore masala dosa :- Same as masala dosa, it contains mixed vegie palya.
  2. Davangere masala dosa :- Same as malasala dosa, it is prepared using wood fire and the batter consistency is little different.
