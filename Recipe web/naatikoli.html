Naati Koli Saaru

Type :- Non-Vegetarian

Style :- South Indian

Cooking Time :- 90 minutes

Best Served With :- Ragi Mudde(Ragi balls), Parota, Chapati etc

Varieties :- Mangalorean Style, Rayalaseema Style , Mandya Style.

Description

Naati Koli Saaru is a traditional chicken curry from Karnataka, India. The term "Naati Koli" translates to "country chicken" or "native chicken," referring to the use of locally grown chickens. This dish is known for its rich and spicy flavor, often enjoyed with ragi mudde (finger millet dumplings), steamed rice, roti, chapati or dosa.

Ingredients

  1. 1 kg Naati Koli (Country Chicken).
  2. 4 cups water (adjust as per requirement).
  3. Salt to taste.
  4. From here the items for masala starts :- 2 big onions.
  5. 1 small tomato.
  6. 15 garlic pods.
  7. 2-inch ginger.
  8. 2 green chilies.
  9. 1/4 tsp turmeric powder
  10. 1 tsp peppercorns.
  11. 4 cloves.
  12. 2-inch cinnamon stick.
  13. 1 cardamom pod.
  14. 4 tbsp oil.
  15. 1/2 cup mint leaves.
  16. 1/2 cup coriander leaves.
  17. 4 tbsp coriander powder.
  18. 2 tsp red chili powder.
  19. 1 tsp garam masala powder.
  20. 1/2 cup frozen peas (optional).
  21. 3 potatoes (optional).
  22. 1/4 cup freshly chopped coriander leaves and mint leaves.

Procedure

  1. Prepare the Masala Paste: Dry roast the spices (coriander seeds, cardamom, fennel seeds, cinnamon, cloves, and dried red chilies) and grind them to a fine powder. Blend the onions, tomatoes, garlic, ginger, green chilies, turmeric powder, peppercorns, cloves, cinnamon stick, and cardamom pods with oil to make a paste.
  2. Cook the Chicken: Marinate the chicken with the masala paste and set aside for about 30 minutes. Heat oil in a pan, add the marinated chicken, and cook until the chicken is browned
  3. Add the Masala: Add the ground masala paste to the chicken and cook until the oil separates. Add water, salt, and bring to a boil Simmer until the chicken is cooked through and the flavors are well combined.
  4. Finish and Serve: Garnish with freshly chopped coriander and mint leaves. Serve hot with steamed rice, ragi mudde, or traditional South Indian breads

Serve With

  1. Ragi mudde(Ragi balls).
  2. Rice.
  3. Parota.
  4. Chapati.
  5. Round chopped onions.
  6. Round chopped cucumber.
  7. Small amount of lime juice.

Variants

  1. Mangalorean Style Koli Saaru :- Uses Byadagi chillies and coconut.
  2. Rayalaseema Style Nati Koli Saaru :- Includes a mix of spices like poppy seeds and grated coconut.
  3. Mandya Style Naati Koli Saaru :- Features a slightly different spice blend and cooking method.
