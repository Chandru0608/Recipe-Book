Upma

Type :- Vegetarian

Style :- South Indian

Cooking Time :- 30 minutes

Best Served With :- Coconut Chutney, Sambar etc

Varieties :- Dappa Rava(Bansi Rava) Upma, Chiroti Rava Upma.

Description

Upma is a popular South Indian breakfast dish made from semolina (rava) and seasoned with spices. It's a savory, comforting dish that's quick to prepare.

Ingredients

  1. 1 cup rava (semolina).
  2. 2 cups Water.
  3. 1 onion, finely chopped.
  4. 1-2 green chilies, chopped.
  5. 1/2 tea spoon mustard seeds.
  6. 1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds.
  7. pinch of asafoetida.
  8. 1 spring curry leaves.
  9. 1 tablespoon oil or ghee.
  10. Salt to taste.
  11. Fresh coriander leaves for garnish.

Procedure

  1. Dry roast the semolina in a pan until it turns light golden brown. Keep aside.
  2. Heat oil or ghee in a pan. Add mustard seeds, cumin seeds, and let them splutter.
  3. Add asafoetida, curry leaves, and green chilies. Sauté for a few seconds.
  4. Add chopped onions and sauté until they turn translucent.
  5. Pour in water, add salt, and bring it to a boil.
  6. Gradually add roasted semolina while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  7. Cook until the semolina absorbs all the water and reaches a thick consistency.
  8. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot.

Serve With

  1. Sambar.
  2. Cocunut chutney.

Variants

  1. Vegetable Upma :- Add mixed vegetables like peas, carrots, beans.
  2. Dappa Rava(Bansi Rava) Upma :- Same as Upma, but instead of normal rava put dappa rava(bansi rava).
  3. Vermicelli Upma.
